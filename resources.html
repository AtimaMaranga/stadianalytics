Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about research methods and data analysis

Sample size depends on your research design, expected effect size, statistical power (typically 0.80), and significance level (typically 0.05). For surveys, you'll also need to consider your population size and desired confidence level. Use sample size calculators or consult with our team for specific guidance based on your study parameters.

SPSS is user-friendly with a graphical interface, ideal for beginners and standard statistical tests. R is powerful for advanced statistics and visualization, with excellent research community support. Python is best for large datasets, machine learning, and integration with other systems. Your choice depends on your comfort level, analysis requirements, and future needs.

Obtain informed consent from participants, ensure confidentiality and data protection, minimize risks, ensure voluntary participation with the right to withdraw, obtain ethics approval from relevant committees, and comply with data protection regulations like the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019.

Quantitative research uses numerical data and statistical analysis to test hypotheses and measure relationships. Qualitative research explores experiences, perceptions, and meanings through interviews, focus groups, and observations. Many studies use mixed methods, combining both approaches for comprehensive insights.

Timeline varies based on dataset size, complexity, and scope of analysis. Basic descriptive statistics may take a few days, while complex modeling with large datasets can take several weeks. Data cleaning often takes 50-80% of total analysis time. Contact us for a specific timeline estimate based on your project.
